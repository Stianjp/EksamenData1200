RRI

RRI, responsible research and innovation, is nowadays mandatory to implement in innovation, and for good reason. We have examined two different inventions, both of which have revolutionized their respective fields. RRI is a crucial tool that can contribute to better problem-solving (RRI Tools, 2015).

Oppenheimer

The first atomic weapon, invented in 1945 by Robert Oppenheimer and others, was used in warfare against Hiroshima, causing immense destruction unprecedented in the world (Holtebekk, 2021). Later, the concept of fission was employed to create nuclear power plants, considered to be a viable source of energy. However, this can have significant consequences in the event of an accident, as witnessed in 1986 in Chernobyl when a nuclear reactor melted down (Rosvold & Hofstad, 2022).

Mark Zuckerberg

Zuckerberg's significant innovation connected society socially, especially in the digital realm. It suddenly became much easier to communicate despite significant distances. Everything became more convenient and accessible, including the ability to exploit people's personal information and daily patterns.

RRI Summary

In summary, we have concluded that if inventions had been regulated within the RRI framework, consideration would have been given to both positive and negative consequences. Innovators would have had to take more responsibility for their inventions and the potential impacts of innovation. If this had been implemented 70-80 years ago, the outcome today might have been different. Perhaps the world would be a safer place, or maybe we would be stuck in a world without development?
